A quick reminder before reading: this article only represents the author’s personal views.


========


To be honest, I’m anxious. Not because the models are so strong that they might replace me or anything, but because the frequent overheating of model servers and the continually shrinking quotas have made me realize that the first round of selection from this new era may already have arrived.


If, in the future, human cognition is fitted with an AI exoskeleton, then the gap between someone who can use SOTA models and someone who can’t may be widened dramatically by just that one model.


And that’s exactly what I’m anxious about. If using a SOTA model costs 100u, most people can probably manage it—or at least be willing to pay. But if today’s price is merely the subsidized price, then when it rises to 200u or 400u, will most people still be willing to pay such a steep price for a model that isn’t guaranteed to improve their lives, and treat it as a necessity? That would be the first filter: with money alone, you can divide people into those who can use SOTA models and those who can’t.


Even though saying this now—when it feels like we’re still in the main surge of the AI hype wave—might be a bit premature, at this point in time it may also count as a “right-side” habit, I guess (


As for why I thought of this, it’s probably because of mythos. I saw what it can do in parts of the security field, and that kind of hit me, making me realize they’re not satisfied with just the coding domain.


And that jolt made me think back to what they’ve done and promoted in the video domain, and their attempts in composition. In fact, they stopped being satisfied with just the code domain a long time ago. So when an industry shifts from promotion into a plateau phase, will the cost of use for users still be as low as it is now?


As someone without much capital, I can only be pushed forward by the times, groping ahead, unsure of right or wrong—maybe that really is tragic. But opportunities in the new world are also spread out in front of everyone; perhaps you could say that this, too, is a gift from the times.
